Anyone who has talked to me in the past few years will know that basically all I do anymore is think and write about the Like A Dragon franchise—think, write, and play, obviously. According to my Xbox, I played just about 737 hours of pure Yakuza in 2024. That might seem like a lot for what the games are, but when you add up all the time I spent teaching myself how to play mahjong or comparing sidewalk textures across the studio’s different game engines, it starts to make sense. During Yakuza 6 I got really into walking. As in walking around. The map. Instead of running. The hours add up quickly.
I say this not just to set up my own personal expertise on this very cool topic, but to emphasize that above all, I am a huge fan of these games. I am a Yakuza fan first and media scholar second. And although there is certainly plenty of media theory (and otherwise) here, I'm not going to hit you with some explicit argument about the state of modern video games. I want to walk you through the streets of Kamurocho. I want you to see through my eyes. Click through my neural pathways. Become a girl who loves things through the internet. Regardless of whether or not anyone can actually know someone else's experience (I'm not going to answer this question here), I do want you to know mine. Consider this the welcome mat.
